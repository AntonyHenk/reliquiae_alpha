This page catalogues basic metadata for all known relic lists with a close connection to medieval English ecclesiastical history. It was initially based on 'Appendix I: A Catalogue of Relic-Lists from English Religious Houses', cataloguing initially done by I. G. Thomas in his dissertation 'The Cult of Saints' Relics in Medieval England' (University of London, 1974, pp. 341-350). Relic lists are organised alphabetically by monastic house or locale. I have based my textual ID system on Thomas' sigla. Textual IDs marked with an asterisk are my own but based on his siglum conventions. This list is also heavily indebted to Julian Luxford's 'The Nature and Purpose of Medieval Relic Lists', in Saints and Cults in Medieval England: Proceedings of the 2015 Harlaxton Symposium (Donington: Shaun Tyas, 2017): 58-79. Please note that this tabular catalogue is continually updated, refined, and extended as my own work continues. You will notice that this catalogue does not currently incorporate resting place lists, such as the Old English 'Secgan þe'; a second tabular catalogue of these texts is planned for a later date. I attempt to provide links to manuscripts and printed editions when possible, but it has not always been possible to link directly to the appropriate page or folio. A link to the relevant web catalogue page may be provided if no digitised version of a manuscript is available. If I have finished an alpha version of a text's web edition, the textual ID will appear as a link; click it to be directed straight to the web edition. For further discussion or notes on manuscript context, dating, etc., click the [§] at the end of the Manuscript and Source Description.
