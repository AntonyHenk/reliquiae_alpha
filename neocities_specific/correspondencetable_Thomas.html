The textual IDs for each item are systematic, and attempt to account for the nesting that is common in relic lists. They work in the following way:

Example: WRC1.A.a*

WRC: Windsor Royal Chapel (taken from I. G. Thomas's catalogue when possible)
1: Indicates that the item comes from a particular manuscript or group of MSS; '1' being the number assigned to the main MS and matching Loca Sanctorum's MS sigla thus: <xml>xml:id="WRC1"</xml>. Furhter witnesses to the same text, when present, are assigned additional numbers.
A: Indicates the boundaries of an 'encompassing' textual item, if more than one are present. For example, this manuscript contains a number of sacrist's inventories for various years, which are best considered distinct texts; the different sacrist's inventories have each been given a letter. This is usually demarcated by apparent large passages of time, combined with evidence from headings and hands.
a: Indicates the boundaries of a 'sub-list', if more than one are present. For example, this manuscript's sacrist's inventories contain sub-lists that specifically refer to the contents of different altars. Sub-list labelling is only used when the length of the material to be edited makes breaking down the text into smaller pieces desireable.
